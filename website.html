Exam Practice Strategy
The AP Chem exam = 60 MCQ (50%) + 7 FRQ (50%) • 3 hrs 15 min total

🎯 How the Exam is Structured

Section I: 60 multiple choice questions in 105 minutes (no calculator for first 15). Section II: 7 free response questions in 105 minutes (calculator allowed). FRQs are graded on reasoning and setup — partial credit is generous, so always show work and explain your reasoning.

✍️ FRQ Tips for a 5

  • 💡 Always write units on every number — points are lost for missing units
  • 💡 "Justify" or "explain" = full sentences. Never just give a number.
  • 💡 If you don't know, attempt anyway — partial credit is very common
  • 💡 Memorize the AP reference table structure — you get it on exam day
  • 💡 For lab-based questions: always link observations to particle-level explanations
  • 💡 Use the correct AP language: "strong acid," "fully dissociates," "rate-determining step"

📅 Practice Test Timeline

  • 📆 Week 3–4: Do unit-specific MCQ on AP Classroom after each unit
  • 📆 Week 5: Attempt past FRQs from 2022 or 2023 (open-note first)
  • 📆 Week 6: Full timed mock exam (2019 released exam)
  • 📆 Week 7: Review mistakes, targeted FRQ practice on weak units
